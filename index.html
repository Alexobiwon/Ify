Move

Monday to Friday, low-impact. Cardio to burn fat, glute & hip work to keep your curves, and calm sessions that are kind to blood pressure.

🌿 Weekend

Rest, or take a relaxed 20–30 minute stroll. Recovery is when the body actually gets stronger — and it helps blood pressure too.

Eat

Real Nigerian food, lighter on salt and oil, built for steady weight loss. Nothing exotic to buy — just everyday meals, cooked a little smarter.

🍲 House rules

  • 1
    Tame the cubes. Go easy on Maggi/Knorr — it's the biggest hidden salt. Flavour with onion, garlic, ginger, scent leaf, uziza, curry, thyme & fresh pepper. Iru and crayfish in small amounts.
  • 2
    Less salt on the plate — taste before you reach for the shaker.
  • 3
    Moderate the palm oil. A little for flavour, not a flood.
  • 4
    Smaller swallow. Half a wrap of eba/amala/fufu; fill the plate with soup, veg & protein instead.
  • 5
    Brown beats white. Ofada or brown rice over white when you can.
  • 6
    Grill, boil, steam, roast — limit deep-fried dodo, akara, puff-puff & chin chin.
  • 7
    Pile on veg — ugu, efo, water leaf, okra, garden egg, tomatoes, peppers.
  • 8
    Lean proteins. Fish (great for the heart), skinless chicken, turkey, eggs, beans, moi moi. Easy on red meat, ponmo & very salty dried fish.
  • 9
    Potassium friends help lower BP — plantain, beans, leafy greens, oranges, tomatoes, avocado pear (ube).
  • 10
    Drink water. Limit sugary minerals, malt & alcohol.

⏱️ Sunday prep, easy week

  • Batch-steam a tray of moi moi and boil a pot of beans — grab-and-go protein.
  • Blend a big base of pepper, tomato, onion (no salt yet) for soups and sauces all week.
  • Grill or boil fish and chicken in batches; store in the fridge.
  • Par-boil brown/ofada rice and pre-cut veg so weeknight cooking is quick.

Care

High blood pressure means a few simple guardrails. They keep movement safe and effective.

✅ Before you start

  • Get a quick OK from your doctor before beginning a new routine.
  • Keep taking any prescribed blood-pressure medication as directed.
  • Know your usual numbers, and check on days you feel off.

🫁 Every session

  • Warm up 5 minutes, cool down 5 minutes — this prevents sudden pressure swings.
  • Breathe steadily. Never hold the breath when straining — breathe out on the effort.
  • Keep a "talk-test" pace: able to chat in short sentences, not gasping.
  • Sip water throughout, and avoid the hottest part of the day.
  • Skip it and rest if you feel unwell, very tired, or your reading is high that day.

🚨 Stop and get help if

During or after exercise, any of these means stop right away and seek medical care:

  • Chest pain, pressure or tightness
  • Pain spreading to the arm, jaw or neck
  • Severe shortness of breath
  • Dizziness, fainting or sudden weakness
  • A sudden pounding headache or blurred vision
  • A racing or irregular heartbeat

🥗 Food & blood pressure

  • Lower salt is the single biggest lever — even halving it helps.
  • More veg and fruit, less fried and processed.
  • Aim for steady, gentle weight loss (about ½–1 kg a week). Slow lasts.

This app is general wellness guidance, not medical advice, diagnosis or treatment. It doesn't replace your doctor. Any medication, and any specific salt or calorie targets, should follow your doctor's advice. If you ever feel seriously unwell, seek medical help.
